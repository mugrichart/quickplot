# Quickplot

A random-driven storytelling tool designed to eliminate planning
paralysis and help writers start writing immediately

Many writers want to write but get stuck planning plots, characters, and
story arcs before the first sentence is even written. Quickplot removes
that barrier by letting **randomized mechanics drive story decisions**,
allowing writers to focus purely on writing.

Instead of planning everything upfront, writers follow prompts and
randomized outcomes that guide the narrative forward.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Problem

Planning can become a form of procrastination.

Writers often spend hours: - outlining plots - designing characters -
deciding who lives or dies - figuring out what should happen next

Quickplot reduces this friction by **automating narrative decisions**,
allowing writers to move directly into storytelling.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Idea

Quickplot uses simple randomness to guide storytelling.

Instead of asking the writer to plan everything, the system decides
things like:

-   which character gets the spotlight
-   which event happens next
-   where the story shifts focus
-   which conflict escalates
-   unexpected twists

The writer's job is simply to **interpret the result and write the
scene**.

This keeps momentum high and removes the mental barrier of "figuring out
what happens next"

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Principles

**1. Writing first**\
The goal is to get words on the page as quickly as possible.

**2. Momentum over perfection**\
Fast drafting is more important than perfect plotting.

**3. Randomness fuels creativity**\
Unexpected prompts often lead to more interesting stories.

**4. Reduce procrastination**\
The system eliminates many of the decisions that cause writers to stall.
